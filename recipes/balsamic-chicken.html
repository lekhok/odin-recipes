Balsamic Grilled Chicken Salad

Description:

Elevate your dining experience with the Balsamic Grilled Chicken Salad. This vibrant creation marries the satisfying heartiness of grilled chicken with the refreshing embrace of a garden-fresh salad. The drama begins as succulent chicken breasts are bathed in a balsamic vinegar marinade, infusing them with a tangy depth of flavor. The grill sizzles as the marinated chicken cooks to juicy perfection, acquiring those enticing charred grill marks. As the chicken rests, a symphony of flavors comes together in the salad bowl. Crisp mixed salad greens provide the backdrop for a colorful canvas, adorned with halved cherry tomatoes that burst with sweetness, slices of cucumber offering a cool crunch, and slivers of red onion providing a hint of sharpness. The grilled chicken, sliced with precision, is the crowning glory. A sprinkling of crumbled feta cheese adds a creamy, tangy element, while chopped walnuts contribute a delightful nutty crunch. The final flourish comes in the form of a balsamic glaze, drizzled artfully over the salad, enhancing its beauty and flavor. Balsamic Grilled Chicken Salad invites you to savor a symphony of textures, tastes, and colors.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 6 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cucumber, sliced
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts

Steps

  1. In a bowl, whisk together balsamic vinegar, olive oil, Dijon mustard, honey, salt, and black pepper to create the marinade.
  2. Place the chicken breasts in a resealable plastic bag and pour in half of the marinade. Seal the bag and let the chicken marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Grill the marinated chicken for about 6-7 minutes per side, or until cooked through.
  4. Remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing it.
  5. In a large salad bowl, combine the mixed salad greens, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, and red onion.
  6. Drizzle the remaining marinade over the salad and toss to coat the greens.
  7. Divide the salad among serving plates, top with sliced grilled chicken, crumbled feta cheese, and chopped walnuts.
