Assam's Masala Milk Tea

Description:

Indulge in the rich and aromatic experience of Masala Milk Tea, a beloved Indian beverage that combines the comfort of warm milk with the invigorating flavors of spices. This tea is a delightful fusion of traditional Indian spices and the soothing qualities of milk, creating a harmonious balance that's both comforting and refreshing. The blend of spices, known as "masala," infuses the tea with a complex and captivating aroma, making each sip a journey through the vibrant tapestry of Indian flavors.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 cup water
  • 2-3 teaspoons loose black tea leaves or tea bags
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 whole cloves
  • 1 small piece of fresh ginger, peeled and sliced
  • Sugar or sweetener to taste

Steps

  1. In a saucepan, combine the milk and water and bring it to a gentle simmer over medium heat.
  2. Add the green cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, whole cloves, and sliced ginger to the milk-water mixture.
  3. Continue to simmer the mixture for about 5-7 minutes, allowing the spices to infuse their flavors into the liquid.
  4. Add the black tea leaves or tea bags to the simmering mixture. If using loose tea leaves, you can use a tea infuser or strain the tea later.
  5. Let the tea simmer for an additional 3-5 minutes, depending on your desired strength. You can adjust the steeping time to achieve the level of tea flavor you prefer.
  6. Stir in sugar or sweetener to taste. The sweetness can be adjusted based on your preference.
  7. Once the tea is ready, strain it into cups to remove the tea leaves and whole spices.
  8. Serve the Masala Milk Tea hot and enjoy the soothing blend of milk and spices.
